How it works

From Lovable Cloud to Supabase in three clear stages.

A temporary helper gives Nativize read-only access to your source. You can inspect it, deploy it, and remove it when the migration is complete.

1

Connect Lovable

Generate a unique helper key in your browser, deploy the transparent read-only helper, and test the connection.

2

Connect Supabase

Paste the connection string for a fresh target project. Credentials stay in your active browser session.

3

Review and move

Confirm the plan, watch live progress, test the new backend, and switch only when you are ready.

FAQ

Before you start.

Do you store my keys?

No. The helper and target credentials are used for the active transfer and are never stored or logged by Nativize.

Will users have to reset their passwords?

No. Auth users move with their password hashes intact, so existing credentials continue to work.

What gets migrated?

Your public database schema and rows, row-level security policies, auth users, and storage buckets and files. A final checklist covers edge functions and provider settings.

Is my Lovable project changed?

No. The source remains read-only and online. You decide when to point your app at the new Supabase backend.
